What is Lua?

Lua is a scripting language that prides itself on its speed. The language is embeddable and supports procedural, object-oriented, functional, and data-driven programming as well as data description.

History of Lua

Lua was created in 1993 by Roberto Ierusalimschy, Luiz Henrique de Figueiredo, and Waldemar Celes, members of the Computer Graphics Technology Group (TeCGraf) at PUC-Rio in Brazil. Lua evolved from internal languages like SOL and DEL, and by version 5.0 in 2003, it introduced a standalone interpreter and virtual machine. Lua has since been adopted globally, especially in games and embedded systems.

Lua's Advantages

Speed

Lua is often the fastest interpreted scripting language in benchmarks. LuaJIT boosts performance even more with just-in-time compilation.

Portability

Lua is small and compiles on virtually any system with a C compiler. It's used across Windows, Linux, iOS, Android, embedded chips, and even Lego Mindstorms.

Embeddable

Lua integrates easily with other languages using a small, clean API. It's frequently used to embed scripting into C/C++ applications.

Powerful

Lua uses meta-mechanisms rather than hardcoded features, making it flexible. While not class-based by default, it supports object-oriented design patterns.

Small

The full Lua distribution is tiny—just a few hundred kilobytes. This makes it ideal for performance-sensitive and embedded applications.

Free

Lua is free open-source software under the MIT license, meaning you can use it for any purpose without cost.
